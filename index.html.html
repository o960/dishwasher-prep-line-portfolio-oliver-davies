Case Studies

A) Weekend Workload — Dexterous Dishwashing

Situation:
Busy weekend night in a high-volume restaurant; heavy returns of pots, pans, utensils, and woks created a soil-intensive backlog at the dish pit.
Task:
Clear high-grime, hard-to-wash cookware while maintaining safety and sanitation standards and keeping priority items available for the line.
Action:
Deployed a 3-compartment sink plus machine workflow: pre-soaked cookware, applied hot water and detergent/degreaser as labeled, used scrub brushes, scrapers, and soap pads; staged pot/pan racks; verified sanitizer ppm and temperatures; coordinated with BOH to triage urgent items.
Result:
Backlog cleared in approximately 30-40 minutes; overall rewash reduced to 10-15%; maintained clean pans and woks throughout service; minimal breakage and no safety incidents; supervisor routinely signed off on station readiness.

B) Courteous Line Practice — Working in a Busy Line

Situation:
Weekend service with 60+ orders across fry and grill stations; coordinated with expo, FOH, and BOH on ticket flow and substitutions.
Task:
Deep-fry and grill menu items and batch-prep components (e.g., batters) while maintaining cook/hold times, food safety, and a clean, organized line.
Action:
Operated fryers, ovens, and stoves; timed basket drops and grill sequencing; skimmed and rotated oil; verified doneness with a probe thermometer; labeled and rotated batters; communicated with the team to stage plates and prioritize rush tickets; cleaned continuously with sanitizer buckets (ppm verified).
Result:
Most orders sent within target ticket times (e.g., 10–12 minutes); correct doneness and temperatures verified; zero safety incidents; minimal waste; FOH remained stocked and on time.

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

  • Sign in and confirm priorities with the supervisor.

  • Clean as you go; support BOH/FOH and reset stations during and after service.
